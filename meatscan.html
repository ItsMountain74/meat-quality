About the Project

What is
MeatScan?

MeatScan is a deep learning–based system that classifies cow meat as fresh or spoiled using only a visual image — no lab equipment required.

The system was trained on 11,000 high-resolution images captured in real-world environments across Ghana, including open-air markets, butcher shops, and cold storage facilities.

Images were labeled based on observable visual cues — texture, colour, and surface condition — verified under natural lighting by trained data collectors.

MeatScan addresses a critical gap in food safety inspection, especially in low-resource settings where traditional testing is unavailable.
